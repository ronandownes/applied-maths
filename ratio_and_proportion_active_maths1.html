16.5 Ratio and Proportion
Ratios are often used to divide or share quantities.
Worked Example 16.11
A pizza is cut into 14 equal slices and is shared between Alan, Brian and Ciara in the ratio 2 : 2 : 3, respectively.
How many slices does each person get?
Solution

Step 1

Write down the total number of parts:
2 + 2 + 3 = 7 parts

Step 2

Express each person’s share as a fraction of the total number of parts:
Alan    2/7
Brian   2/7
Ciara   3/7

Step 3

Multiply each fraction by the amount to be shared:
Alan:   (2/7) × 14 = 4 slices     Brian:   (2/7) × 14 = 4 slices     Ciara:   (3/7) × 14 = 6 slices

Worked Example 16.12
Jack and Danny find an old money box. They decide to share out the money in the ratio of their ages. Jack is 8 and Danny is 10. If Danny gets €60:
(a) How much was in the money box?
(b) How much did Jack get?
Solution

Step 1

Find what 1 part is equal to:
Danny = 10 parts = €60
10 parts / 10 = €60 / 10
1 part = €6
Divide both sides by 10.

Step 2

Find the total number of parts:
8 parts + 10 parts = 18 parts
1 part × 18 = €6 × 18
∴ 18 parts = €108
Total money = €108
(b) Jack got 8 parts:
€6 × 8 = €48
Proportionality
Two quantities are in direct proportion if, when the numbers involved are changed, their ratios stay the same.
Worked Example 16.13
Allie’s pocket money is in direct proportion to the number of hours of chores done. She gets €12 for every two hours of chores she does.
(a) If Allie helps out at home for three hours, how much will she get?
(b) How many hours would she have to help out for to get €36?
Solution

(a)

Find out what she gets for 1 hour:
2 hours = €12
1 hour = €6
So 3 hours = €6 × 3 = €18
Allie will get €18.

(b)

Find the number of hours she needs to work to get €36:
From part (a):
1 hour = €6
€36 ÷ €6 = 6 hours
Allie will have to help out for 6 hours.

Value for Money Calculations and Judgements

Proportion calculations can be used to decide which items in a shop offer the best value for money. Many items sold in supermarkets display a price, and a price per 100 g (or per kg) on the shelf edge label in front of the product. This lets customers compare products and get the best value for money.

Worked Example 16.14
Chocolate finger biscuits are on offer in two different sizes in Gerry’s local shop. A 140 g pack costs €1.50 while a 230 g pack costs €3.00. Which pack is the better value? Justify your answer.
Solution

Step 1

Find the cost of 100 g for the 140 g pack:
140 g = €1.50
1 g = €1.50 / 140
100 g = (€1.50 / 140) × 100 = €1.07
∴ Price per 100 g is €1.07 (to the nearest cent)

Step 2

Find the cost of 100 g for the 230 g pack:
230 g = €3.00
1 g = €3.00 / 230
100 g = (€3.00 / 230) × 100 = €1.30
∴ Price per 100 g is €1.30 (to the nearest cent)

Step 3

Give your judgement:
The 140 g pack is the better value, as it is cheaper per 100 g.
It can be difficult to make the comparison when decimals are involved, so it’s a good idea to compare using 100 g and round the price to the nearest cent.
Worked Example 16.15
If 1 kg of fusilli pasta costs €3.19, and 750 g of pasta shells costs €1.69, which one is value for money? Justify your answer.
Solution

Step 1

Find the cost of 100 g of fusilli:
(Remember 1 kg = 1,000 g)

1,000 g = €3.19
100 g = €3.19 / 10
100 g = €0.319
∴ Price per 100 g is €0.32 (to the nearest cent)

Step 2

Find the cost of 100 g of pasta shells:
750 g = €1.69
1 g = €1.69 / 750
100 g = (€1.69 / 750) × 100
100 g = €0.225
∴ Price per 100 g is €0.23 (to the nearest cent)
Step 3   Make your judgement.
The 750 g pack of pasta shells is the better value for money, as it is cheaper per 100 g.
Always make sure your units of measurement are the same — either all kg or all g.

16.6 Currency Exchange

In the Eurozone, the unit of currency is the euro (€). If you travel to any country within the Eurozone, there is no need to change your money. However, if you travel to any country outside of the Eurozone, you will need to change your money. For example, if you travel to Japan, you will need to convert your money from euro into Japanese yen.

The exchange rate between two currencies tells you how much one unit of the first currency is worth in units of the second currency.
